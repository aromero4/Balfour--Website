Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Balfour-Hesburgh Program?

The Balfour-Hesburgh Scholars Program (BHSP) is at the forefront of Notre Dame’s commitment to diversity and inclusion in all aspects of our university’s academic and social life.

BHSP seeks to provide high-achieving students from underrepresented populations, including, but not limited to, students from low-income backgrounds and/or under-resourced high schools, or who may be the first in their family to attend college, with: a close-knit learning community of current students at Notre Dame; and a network of successful professionals who are alumni of the program.

How do I apply to BHS?

The application process to become a Balfour-Hesburgh Scholar starts when new first-year students who have been accepted to the University of Notre Dame are invited to apply. BHSP draws from the list of accepted and confirmed students who meet the eligibility criteria and then sends invitations to apply to the program. Our application schedule for the academic year is: March/April — Admissions acceptance letters sent to students. Mid-April — BHSP sends letters of invitation to eligible students. May 6 — Online applications must be submitted by 5:00 pm EST. Late applications will not be considered. Be sure to attach your high school transcripts.

Am I eligible for BHS?

To be considered for the Balfour-Hesburgh Scholars Program (BHSP), eligible incoming Notre Dame first-year students are expected to have completed a strong college preparatory program of study from an accredited high school. The minimum program of study should include: English: four years Social Science/History: three years Mathematics: four years Science: four years Language other than English: two years It is strongly recommended that applicants have completed four years of mathematics, including trigonometry, pre-calculus, and/or calculus if they are interested in majoring in a field of science, technology, engineering, or mathematics. Advanced placement courses and research experience are desired but not required. Competitive applicants to the Balfour-Hesburgh Scholars Program have not received lower than a B in any high school science or math course. Note: Many of our past and current scholars completed a year or more of calculus. Recent successful applicants had: a minimum of 600 on the Math component of the SAT a cumulative high school GPA of a 3.0 or above a demonstrated commitment to community service Balfour-Hesburgh scholars must be citizens of the United States, or have permanent resident or DACA status.

What is the Balfour-Hesburgh Scholars Summer Institute?

During this four-week residential experience, students complete a combination of academic coursework and personal development courses. They will be introduced to the vast resources Notre Dame offers, as well as the level of work that will be required in the fall. The BHSP Summer Institute at Notre Dame is designed to jump-start our Scholars’ transition to college life and their acculturation to the demanding academic environment of a top-20 university.

Does BHS or the Summer Institute cost anything?

BHSP, as well as the Summer Institute comes at completely no cost to students if accepted. BHSP is dedicated to providing the resources and oppurtuinites for it scholars to succeed and thrive at the university and beyond.
